Does Social Media Usage Affect Academic Performance?

Introduction

The purpose of this study is to investigate whether the amount of time students spend on social media impacts their academic performance, as measured by GPA. Our main research question is: "Does social media usage affect student academic performance?" The hypothesis is that students who spend less time on social media have higher GPAs. Alternatively, there may be no significant effect.

In today’s digital age, social media is a major part of students’ lives and could potentially influence time management, study habits, focus, and overall academic success. This project will analyze the data using graphs that compare social media usage (in hours) with the GPA of each student, while also noting whether social media is used during study time.

Data Collection

Data was collected using a Google Form survey distributed to students who were randomly selected from the hallway. The survey included the following questions:

  • How many hours do you spend on social media per day?
  • What is your current GPA?
  • Do you use social media while studying?
  • Year in School

No identifiable information was collected in order to maintain the privacy and ethical integrity of the study. In total, 30 responses were gathered for analysis.

Graphs and Analysis

Scatter Plot: Social Media Hours vs. GPA

This scatter plot shows individual student data points. The x-axis represents the number of hours spent on social media per day, and the y-axis represents the student’s GPA. Data points are color-coded by whether the student uses social media while studying.

Bar Chart: Average GPA by Social Media Usage Group

In this bar chart, students have been divided into three groups based on their social media usage:

  • Low (1-3 hrs)
  • Medium (4-6 hrs)
  • High (7-8 hrs)

The chart displays the average GPA for each group calculated from the survey data:
Low: 3.18 | Medium: 3.24 | High: 2.76

Conclusion

The fact that students who spend less time on social media have higher GPA is partially supported by the data. Students in the high usage group demonstrate lower academic performance compared to their peers in the low and medium social media usage groups. However, the relatively small difference between the low and medium groups implies that moderate social media use may not significantly impact GPA.
